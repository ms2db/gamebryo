Vertex Color Properties and the Lighting Equation

NiVertexColorProperty objects have two independent controls. These controls affect the way that the general Gamebryo Lighting Equation is used to generate final vertex colors.

The first control is the source vertex routing control. This control sets whether the given vertex emissive, ambient and/or diffuse values are specified as constants for the entire surface by the current NiMaterialProperty or changing per-vertex as specified in the source vertex colors of the object. Possible settings are:

  • SOURCE_IGNORE—emissive, ambient and diffuse are constant across an object's surface as specified by the corresponding material colors (in other words, VE=ME, VA=MA, VD=MD).
  • SOURCE_EMISSIVE—the material emissive color is replaced by the source vertex colors (in other words, VE=VC, VA=MA, VD=MD).
  • SOURCE_AMB_DIFF -- the material ambient and diffuse colors are both replaced by the source vertex colors (in other words, VE=ME, VA=VC, VD=VC).

The second control is the lighting control. This control determines which components of the lighting equation are used to determine the final vertex color. Possible settings are:

  • LIGHTING_E -- only the emissive component of the lighting equation is used.
  • LIGHTING_E_A_D -- all three components (emissive, ambient and diffuse) are summed to compute the final vertex color.

In general, LIGHTING_E is used with SOURCE_EMISSIVE to implement pre-lit geometry (lighting "baked" into the vertex colors), while LIGHTING_E_A_D is used with any of the source modes to implement dynamic lighting.
