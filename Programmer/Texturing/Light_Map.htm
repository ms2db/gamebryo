Light Map

Light Maps are RGB maps that are applied, summed together as a group, added to the Dark Map (if any) and then used to modulate the Base Map. As a result, the light maps act very much like Gamebryo vertex lights, in the sense that each additional light adds to all of the others, and the result is used to darken the Base Map. Unlike Dark Maps, they may be used to project textures into the scene, since the Light Maps are projected multitextures. Common Light Map-based effects includes searchlights, slide projectors and film projectors, light shafts from windows (including stained glass), and overhead cloud cover. This is perhaps the most versatile and powerful class of projected texture effects.
