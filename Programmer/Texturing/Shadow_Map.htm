Shadow Map

Shadow Maps are RGB maps that modulate the Base Map. They are designed to be used to project shadow images (either pre-rendered or rendered on-the-fly to a texture) into the scene. Unlike projected lights, where overlapping areas tend to make the scene brighter, projected shadows tend to look darker in areas where more than one of them overlaps. This is reasonable behavior for shadows, and makes realistic shadow-to-shadow interaction possible. The same types of projections may be used with Shadow Maps as with Light Maps, allowing for a similarly wide range of effects.
