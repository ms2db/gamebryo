Clipped Projected Texture Limitations

Clipped projected textures sometimes require extra texture resources than unclipped textures. For full details, please refer to the renderer documentation for specific platforms.

In legacy, fixed-function pipelines, each clipped projected texture requires 2 texture stages at the beginning of a rendering pass of its own, so each clipped projected texture requires a rendering pass, though additional projected lights and shadows can often be rendered in the same pass. In shader-driven pipelines, the clipping is achieved through mathematical instructions in the pixel shaders.

Currently, no Gamebryo platform supports clipped projected environment maps or fog maps, only clipped projected lights and shadows. This should not be a significant limitation, as most environment maps and fog maps do not require clipping.

On platforms that cannot support clipped projected textures, clipped projected textures will simply be rendered without the clipping (i.e. they will be rendered as bi-directional).
