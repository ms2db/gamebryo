Using Gloss Maps

  

Left—no gloss map; Right—with crosshatch gloss map

  • Image: The image for a Gloss Map is as described above in the introduction to Gloss Maps—white in mirror-like areas, black in matte areas, and tinted in metallic areas.
  • Filter: Dependent upon the effect—there are no specific requirements for Gloss Maps.
  • Clamping: Dependent upon the effect.
  • Texture Coordinates: Completely dependent upon the desired effect—gloss map effects are as varied as base texturing. However the coordinates tend to either cause the texture to be mapped once across the surface (representing large-scale glossy and matte regions of the surface), or repeated numerous times across the surface (representing a small-scale texture or bumpiness of the surface).
