Clipping & Culling

The clipping/culling behavior of Gamebryo must be kept in mind while creating scene geometry.

  • Clipping is the process of dividing polygons into only the fragments that will appear on screen.
  • Culling is an attempt to avoid clipping by rejecting whole NiMesh objects if no part of them appears on the screen. In general, culling is preferable to clipping because clipping is vastly more expensive than culling. However, in modern hardware draw calls can be the performance bottleneck, in which case having fewer object becomes desirable.

You can structure your scenes to improve culling by limiting the volume of space an NiMesh object occupies. A small NiMesh object is more likely to be completely off screen while a very large one (for example, a huge floor) is likely to be partially on-screen all the time.
