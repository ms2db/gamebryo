Choosing Your Maya Viewer

As you create your scene in Maya you will want to view it from time to time and see how it will look in Gamebryo. There are five different viewer options:

  • Asset Viewer
  • Gamebryo Animation Tool
  • D3D9 viewer
  • Xbox 360 viewer
  • PS3 viewer
  • D3D10 viewer
  • D3D11 viewer

Export-Display Options

To choose your Maya viewer

  1. In Maya, select "Export All" from the "File" menu. Alternately one could click on the Export Options Button on the Gamebryo Tool Shelf.
  2. In the "Export All" dialog, click the "Options" button.
  3. In the "Export All Options" dialog, set the file type to NIF.
  4. Under the "Export-Display Options" section, select the viewer you wish to use.
    • Asset Viewer—by far the best general viewing tool. It allows you to move the camera, play animations, and see details of the scene graph.
    • Gamebryo Animation Tool—used to view a character and customize the transitions between sequences.

      Note

      If your scene does not contain a character and you have chosen the Animation Tool as your default viewer, the Gamebryo Maya exporter will automatically switch to use the Asset Viewer for viewing.
    • D3D9 Viewer—a simple viewer with FPS-style controls. If you just want a quick glance at your scene, this will be sufficient. However, all the functionality of the D3D9 viewer exists in the Gamebryo Asset Viewer and the D3D9 viewer continues to exist for backward compatibility.
    • Xbox 360 (Optional)—One of the exciting features of the Gamebryo Maya exporter is the option for on-target viewing. The scene will be sent across the network and shown on your Xbox 360 development kit. If you don't have the Gamebryo Xbox 360 viewer, this option will be grayed out.
    • PS3 (Optional)—If you have a PS3 development kit it is possible to use the PS3 on target viewer to see your scene on the PS3. If you don't have a PS3 this option will be grayed out.
    • D3D10—If you have a D3D10-capable computer, it is possible to use the D3D10 viewer to see your scene. If you don't have a D3D10-capable computer, this option will default to using the D3D9 viewer.
    • D3D11—If you have a D3D11-capable computer, it is possible to use the D3D10 viewer to see your scene. If you don't have a D3D11-capable computer, this option will default to using the D3D9 viewer.
  5. In the upper left corner of the window there is a menu option labeled "Edit". Click on the item labeled "Save Settings", and close the Export All Options window.
