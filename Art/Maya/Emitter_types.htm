Emitter types

Direction and Spread Emitter Conflicts

One of the most important things about an Emitter is which direction it is pointing and what kind of directional variation you can have. Gamebryo and Maya use two completely different schemes in this regard. To define the direction of an emitter, Gamebryo uses a "Declination" off of the Y-axis and a "Planar Angle", which is a rotation around the Y-axis. The Gamebryo Maya Plug-in converts the direction from Maya to Gamebryo without any difficulties.

 

Declination and Planar Angle

The difficulties come in to play when defining what the "Spread" of an emitter will be.  For each of the different Maya emitters, "Spread" is defined differently.  Within Gamebryo, "Spread" is defined as a variation of "Declination" and "Planar Angle".  The best compromise available is for the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in to set both variations equal to the "Spread" found in Maya.  Any customizations must be done programmatically.

Directional Emitters

Directional Emitters create particles from a single point.  The best example is a spray bottle where the spray gets created from a single point at the end of the bottle and spreads out in a cone.  Adjusting the "Rate" will change how much spray is created.  The "Direction" indicates which way the bottle is facing.  Adjusting the spread will create everything from a thin stream to wide cloud.  "Speed" indicates how fast each particle is moving when it is initially created.  "Speed Random" will randomly modify the initial "Speed" of each particle.  The "Particle Color" will set the material or texture for each particle.  Finally, the "Translate X,Y,Z" and "Rotate X,Y,Z" will set this emitter's location.

Maya direction emitter attributes include the following:

  • Translate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rotate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rate (Particles/Sec) (animated)
  • Direction X,Y,Z
  • Spread
  • Speed (animated)
  • Speed Random
  • Particle Color

Omni Emitters

Omni Emitters create particles from a single point as well, but they are not associated with a specific direction. They create a uniform spray of particles in all directions around a central point. Each of the Maya attributes operates the same as "Directional Emitters", minus direction control. The sparks from the acetylene torch burst into existence at the torch's tip and scatter in all directions.  They are not affected by gravity, and they will pass through all objects without colliding.

Maya omni emitter attributes include the following:

  • Translate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rotate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rate (Particles/Sec) (animated)
  • Speed (animated)
  • Speed Random
  • Particle
  • Color

Volume Emitters

Volume Emitters create particles within a volume instead of a single point. Gamebryo places an important restriction on what type of volume can be used. The Gamebryo Maya Plug-in can only supports the cube, sphere, and cylinder volume emitters. The emitter attributes work the same as a "Directional Emitter" with the exception of "Scale X,Y,Z", "Directional Speed", and "Random Direction". By setting the "Scale" attribute of the "RainEmitter", you change the size of the volume where the raindrops will be created.

The spread of a "Volume Emitter" is controlled by the ratio of "Random Direction" and "Directional Speed".  When the "Random Direction" is low and the "Directional Speed" is high, then you get a narrow spray. When the "Random Direction" is high and the "Directional Speed" is low, then you get a wide spray.

Maya volume emitter attributes include the following:

  • Translate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rotate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Scale X, Y, Z (animated)
  • Rate (Particles/Sec) (animated)
  • Direction X,Y,Z
  • Speed Random
  • Directional Speed (animated)
  • Random Direction
  • Particle Color

Surface emitters

Surface Emitters spawn particles from a mesh surface. You can specify the location where particles emit from. This includes specifying a random vertex as well as a random location on the triangle surface. It is important to remember that there are performance differences between each of the emit locations. Emitting from a random vertex is less expensive then choosing a random spot on a triangle face.

Maya surface emitter attributes include the following:

  • Translate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rotate X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Scale X,Y,Z (animated)
  • Rate (Particles/Sec) (animated)
  • Direction (Surface Normal, Random, or Directional
  • Speed Random
  • Direction Speed (animated)
  • Particle Color
  • Emit Location (Vertices, Face Center, Edge Center, Face Surface, Edge Surface)
