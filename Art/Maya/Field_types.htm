Field types

Within Maya, "Fields" are forces that affect particles. Of the many different types of fields found within Maya, the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in supports Gravity, Radial, Turbulence, Air, Vortex, and Drag fields. As is found in Maya, the different fields in Gamebryo now reference an external "field" object in the scene graph. By animating this object you can animate the positional affects of these fields. For example, you could animate the air field to simulate the changing direction of wind. All fields contain three attributes; Magnitude, Attenuation, and Maximum Distance.

Gravity fields

Within Maya, "Gravity" fields are forces acting along the "Direction" vector. This gravity force will pull down all particles to which the force is connected. "Magnitude" indicates the strength of the field. "Attenuation" controls how the field is affected by distance to the gravity field object. Finally, "Direction" controls which way the field will pull the particles. There is an important fundamental difference between Gamebryo and Maya with respect to the "Direction" of gravity. In Maya the direction of gravity is a world space value and does not change even if you rotate the gravity object in Maya. In Gamebryo, the direction of gravity is with respect to the field object by it is not considered a world space value. By animating the rotation of the gravity field you will change the direction of gravity.

Maya gravity field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance
  • Direction XYZ

Radial fields

"Radial" fields within Maya pull or push particles away from a central point. The "Magnitude" of the force controls how much power is exerted on the particles. "Attenuation" will modify the force of the "Radial" field with distance.

Maya radial field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance

Turbulence fields

Turbulence fields in Maya randomly adjust the velocity of a particle. The "Magnitude" of the force controls show much the particle velocity changes. The effect of turbulence is also controlled by the attenuation and the maximum distance. The frequency of a turbulence field determines how many times per second the turbulence is applied to all particles.

Maya turbulence field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance
  • Frequence

Drag fields

Drag fields in Maya dampen the movement of object. The Magnitude of the force controls the strength of the dampening effect. Attenuation and Maximum Distance are used to determine the final strength of the field.

Maya drag field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance
  • Rotation XYZ

Air fields

Air fields in Maya simulate the affects of moving air on particles. The particles are accelerated up the Magnitude (velocity).

Maya air field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance
  • Inherit Velocity
  • Inherit Rotation
  • Speed
  • Use Spread
  • Spread

By setting the attributes you can create three very different types of effects: wind, fans, or wake.

  • Wind
    • Magnitude = wind speed
    • Inherit Velocity = 0.0
    • Inherit Rotation = false
    • Use Spread = false
  • Fan
    • Magnitude = air speed
    • Inherit Velocity = 0.5
    • Inherit Rotation = true
    • Use Spread = true
  • Wake
    • Magnitude = 0.0
    • Inherit Velocity = 1.0
    • Inherit Rotation = true
    • Use Spread = false

Vortex fields

Vortex fields in Maya simulate the affects of vortex on particles. The particles are accelerated around the directional axis by the Magnitude (velocity). Attenuation and Maximum Distance are used to determine the final strength of the field.

Maya vortex field attributes include the following:

  • Magnitude
  • Attenuation
  • Use Max Distance
  • Max Distance
  • Direction
