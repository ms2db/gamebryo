Trees Ablend vs ATest 02.max

The Trees Ablend vs ATest 02.max sample illustrates different ways to apply transparency to foliage, specifically focusing on Alpha blending vs. Alpha testing.

One common use of textured transparency is with billboard or cross plane trees. The two trees are shaded by the materials "Alpha Blend Mat" and "Alpha Test Mat," with the respective assignment of material to individual tree. Running the sample displays two identical trees, except for their transparency. The alpha blended tree has softer edges than the alpha tested tree, but the alpha blended tree flickers because of sorting conflicts. For this reason, alpha testing is the recommended alternative for this type of situation.

Note

These materials have their "Apply Mode" option changed from MODULATE to REPLACE. This approach was adopted to eliminate any artifacts from the default Max lighting. REPLACE mode instruct Gamebryo to ignore both vertex colors and dynamic lights and is usually used when all necessary lighting has been included in the textures or will be provided via dark maps.

With alpha testing, pixels that fail the alpha test are not written to the frame buffer. To further demonstrate the behavior of alpha testing, try changing the "Test Mode" of the "Alpha Test Mat" from GREATER to LESSEQUAL. This setting will show those pixels that were being eliminated earlier. It should appear as a halo around the tree. If you also change the transparency mode of the material to "Standard", the pixels that are totally transparent on smalloakfour.tga will not appear for either setting of "Test Mode". In one case, they are ignored. In another case, they are alpha blended into the scene.
