Creating an Idle Sequence

Take your character out of Figure Mode and pose it the way it should be standing idle. Select all the bones in the biped. Then go under the Motion panel Keyframing rollout panel and depress the Copy Posture key. Change it to Copy Pose. Then press the Animate button, select the correct keyframe, and click the Paste Posture button in the Keyframing rollout panel. It will give you a warning window. Answer Yes. This procedure ensures that keys are placed in all the transform tracks, not just one. If you try a simpler way, such as selecting the entire figure and right-clicking on the animation bar to set a key, it will not set keys in all the transform tracks. When adding Note Tracks for Idle Sequences, your start key should be at zero, and your end key should be any other key except zero.

When extracting the KF sequences, make sure that you check the box to "use existing biped keyframes", otherwise your results will vary.
