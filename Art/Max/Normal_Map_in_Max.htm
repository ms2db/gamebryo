Normal Map in Max

The "Normal" map field holds a texture that encodes the normals of a piece of geometry into RGB values. This is often used to add lighting details on low polygon count objects, generated from higher polygon count versions of the same object. It is important to note that all normal maps are tangent-space in the Gamebryo standard pipeline. This means the Normal, Binormal, and Tangent (NBT) frames must be calculated per-vertex. This calculation will be performed automatically when you assign a normal map texture.

Assigning a normal map to an object will cause lighting calculations, including specularity to be performed on a per-pixel basis. The normals from the normal map will also affect the texture lookup into an environment map.
