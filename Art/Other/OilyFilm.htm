OilyFilm

<OilyFilm.NSF>

Requirements

  • NSF Shader Library
  • Vertex Shader 1.1 and Pixel Shader 1.4

Description

This shader uses two lights to illuminate the object with both diffuse and specular channels. The diffuse channel is used to modulate the base map. The specular channel is used to modulate a lookup into a 1D ramp that represents an oily film effect on the surface of the object. This lookup is based on a dot product calculation of the normal and the view vector. The final warp effect is sampled in the U axis by the same normal and view vector dot product and sampled in the V axis by the warpAlpha attribute.

Art Requirements

  • Base map (Using first UV set)
  • Two lights of any kind

Custom Attributes

  • Exponent—This is the specular exponent for the effect.
  • WarpAlpha—The V coordinate for the warpEffect lookup.
  • filmRamp—The 1D ramp texture for the thin film interference effect. The texture should be approximately the interference pattern for thin film interactions where increasing U is increasing thickness of the film.
  • warpEffect—The 2D texture whose U axis indicates a 1D ramp of transparency based off of the normal dotted with the view vector. The V axis indicates increasing time.

Global Variables

There are no global variables in this shader.

Object Variables

  • Light1—A generic light whose diffuse color and direction are used to light the model.
  • Light2—A generic light whose diffuse color and direction are used to light the model.
