Second, Consider ABVs for 3D Collision Detection

Consider an ABV approach if you need 3D collision detection.

Collision detection can be an expensive operation in a real-time 3D application. In particular, triangle-triangle collision detection is very expensive, given the number of triangles in modern datasets. Often you won't need that level of detail on the collision, especially since its cost is high.

In cases where a character is involved, an optimal 3D collision solution (although a 2D approach may be faster) may be to work directly with Alternate Bounding Volume (ABV) collisions, without propagating the collision detection to triangles lower in the hierarchy (i.e., below the ABVs).

You can increase performance significantly by carefully designing your application to take advantage of specific domain knowledge, using the ABV system with the various propagation flags. In most cases, the preferred approach will be a combination of extensive use of ABVs, coupled with frequent use of the PROPAGATE_NEVER propagation flag.

Where greater accuracy is desired, simpler geometry in the form of a proxy may be created to provide the level of detail needed for collision detection. For example, it is conceivable that a 5000-triangle object may be modeled effectively as a 100-triangle proxy, where the proxy effectively approximates the appropriate collision detection behavior.

By providing a variety of ABV types, propagation flags, and collision proxies, Gamebryo offers a great deal of flexibility, so application developers can find the right combinations for optimal performance and effective collision response.

You may use the ABV construction facilities of the Gamebryo exporter plug-ins to create ABVs that may be streamed to the same Nif file that contains your model scene graph. This approach involves tagging the geometry with special names that the CreateABV plug-in will use in deciding what ABVs to create.
