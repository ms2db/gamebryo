Room Groups

A visibility graph is the high-level representation of an interior scene in the portals module. This visibility graph describes the "topology" of the rooms within the scene. If drawn as a graph, nodes of the graph are the rooms and the edges (directed) are the portals. This represents the idea that the only way of seeing out of a room (and into another) is through a doorway, window, or opening in the wall of the room.

The entire interior scene is represented in Gamebryo with an NiRoomGroup object. This room group manages the rooms in its interior scene, and "directs" the culling of the rooms. Its main purpose is to start the culling of the graph of rooms from the correct room. In this case, the correct room is the room (if any) that contains the current camera location. When a room group is tested for visibility, it determines the room (if any) that contains the camera, and recursively processes that room. If no room contains the camera's location, then the room group assumes that the camera is currently located outside the interior scene, and processes the "shell" or exterior representation of the scene, if one is supplied. If there is no shell and the camera does not fall within a room in the room group, then the room that most recently contained the camera is used.
