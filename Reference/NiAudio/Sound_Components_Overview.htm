Sound Components Overview

Implementing 3D audio in a Gamebryo application minimally requires three components: the audio system core, the listener object, and at least one sound source.

The audio system core, referred to hereinafter as simply the audio system, serves as a control center for audio rendering. It can control global audio parameters as well as advanced audio functionality.

The listener represents the single audience for sound in the application; typically, this single audience is the application's user. It defines the listener's location and orientation in the 3D space of the application.

Lastly, audio sources control playback of digital audio data in the form of wave or other encoded files. Sources have multiple control options for individual audio playback. Like the listener, they also have location and orientation in 3D space. In order to mirror these requirements three classes make up the NiAudio library. These classes are:

  • NiAudioSystem
  • NiAudioListener
  • NiAudioSource
