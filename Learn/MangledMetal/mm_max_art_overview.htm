MangledMetal 3ds Max art assets overview

All source art assets can be found under: %EMERGENT_PATH%\Samples\GameDemos\MangledMetal\Art Files. The vast majority of source art assets reside in the Export Folder and were created in either 3ds Max 9 or Maya 8.5. Assets in the Export_Manual folder are either platform-specific or were authored in 3D Studio Max 2009. Assets in the Export_Manual folder should not be exported as part of a batch export, and instead must exported individually.

Walkable/Nonwalkable

In order to work with the MangledMetal picking service, all MangledMetal assets contain one or more meshes called Walkable or NonWalkable. This allows an artist to define in the scene file where players cannot walk, or what they will walk on top of.

To work properly, these meshes must:

  • Have the PosNormPicking Mesh Packing Profile applied
  • Be hidden
  • Be named either "Walkable" or "NonWalkable"
If there are multiple NonWalkable or Walkable meshes that make up the desired area, they need to be grouped under a single node named either "Walkable" or "NonWalkable" (Just grouping multiple meshes and calling the group"Walkable" or "NonWalkable" works fine.

A good example of the NonWalkable prop can be found under %EMERGENT_PATH%\Samples\GameDemos\MangledMetal\Art Files\Export\Props\Door04_MMGate.max. You will need to 'Unhide all' to see the NonWalkable group.

It is important to note that the picking service calculates from the center of the character. As such, all NonWalkable meshes should extend beyond the edge of their associated mesh by approximately the same distance as the radius of the character (about 150 centimeters for the Player Character).

PhysX

All assets that interact with the PhysX simulation (as either active or passive rigid bodies) also have an associated Rigid Body in the scene file. It is important to note that, once exported, these Rigid Bodies will not scale along with their parent geometry inside of World Builder.
