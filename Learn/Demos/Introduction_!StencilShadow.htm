Introduction to the StencilShadow Demo

The StencilShadow demo shows how to use the stencil buffer to create shadow effects on hardware and buffer configurations that can support stencil buffering. Stencil buffering shadows work best with simple (or static) casters casting shadows on very complex recipients. For example, stencil buffer-based shadows are a good system to use for shadows cast by a building or post on a complex skinned character. They do not work as well or as efficiently when complex, morphing characters are used as casters, as the extruded shadow volume must be represented by a 3D polyhedron of triangles.  In those cases, techniques such as projected shadows (demonstrated in the StandardMaterial demo) or shadow maps (shown in the ShadowMap demo) should be used.

Note that this demo will draw a very dark scene (almost black) on 3D hardware or buffer configurations that do not support stencil buffering.
