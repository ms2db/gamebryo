Asset Viewer Camera Navigation Toolbar

You can use the Camera Navigation Toolbar to select objects in the scene, manipulate the current camera, switch between cameras, and switch rendering modes.

Camera interaction modes

Camera interaction modes determine how the mouse and keyboard will control the current camera.

Object selection mode

Highlights objects and selects them in the Scene Graph Tree View and Scene Graph List View when they are clicked in the Render View.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—selects the object in the scene that is currently pointed to.
  • 3D Studio MAX:
    • ALT + middle mouse button—Orbits camera around current selection.
    • Middle mouse button—Pans the camera.
    • Wheel—Zooms the camera.
    • Focus changes when a camera movement occurs after a change in selection.
  • Maya:
    • ALT + left mouse button—Orbits camera around current selection.
    • ALT + middle mouse button—Pans the camera.
    • ALT + right mouse button—Zooms the camera.
    • Wheel—Zooms the camera.
    • F key—Updates the camera to focus on the current selected object.

Pan mode

Moves the camera based off the current axis constraints.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—camera movement based off of current axis constraint
  • Wheel—zoom in and out

Keyboard:

  • W—Move the camera backwards
  • A—Move the camera left
  • S—Move the camera backwards
  • D—Move the camera right
  • R—Move the camera up
  • F—Move the camera down

Collision mode

User input is now used to test collisions in the world.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—Move collider based off of current axis constraint
  • Wheel—Move collider forwards and backwards

Keyboard:

  • W—Move the collider up
  • A—Move the collider left
  • S—Move the collider down
  • D—Move the collider right
  • R—Move the collider forwards
  • F—Move the collider backwards

Rotate mode

Rotates the camera in place based off the current axis constraints.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—camera rotation based off of current axis constraint
  • Wheel—zoom in and out

Keyboard:

  • W—Move the camera backwards
  • A—Move the camera left
  • S—Move the camera backwards
  • D—Move the camera right
  • R—Move the camera up
  • F—Move the camera down
  • UP—Pitch the camera up
  • DOWN—Pitch the camera down
  • LEFT—Yaw the camera to the left
  • RIGHT—Yaw the camera to the right

Tumble mode

Orbits the camera about the scene graph center based off of the current axis constraints.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—camera tumbling based off of current axis constraint
  • Wheel—zoom in and out

Keyboard:

  • W—Move the camera backwards
  • A—Move the camera left
  • S—Move the camera backwards
  • D—Move the camera right
  • R—Move the camera up
  • F—Move the camera down
  • UP—Pitch the camera up
  • DOWN—Pitch the camera down
  • LEFT—Yaw the camera to the left
  • RIGHT—Yaw the camera to the right

Zoom mode

Simulates zooming by moving the camera forwards and backwards.

Mouse:

  • Left mouse button—zoom in and out
  • Wheel—zoom in and out

Keyboard:

  • W—Move the camera backwards
  • A—Move the camera left
  • S—Move the camera backwards
  • D—Move the camera right
  • R—Move the camera up
  • F—Move the camera down

Axis Constraints

Axis constraints limit the movement of the camera for the pan, rotate, and tumble camera interaction modes.

  • X Axis Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement only in the X-axis of the camera, or its "right" vector. The modifier key for this constraint is 'X.'
  • Y Axis Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement only in the Y-axis of the camera, or its "up" vector. The modifier key for this constraint is 'Shift.'
  • Z Axis Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement only in the Z-axis of the camera, or its "forward" vector. The modifier key for this constraint is 'Control.'
  • XY Axes Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement relative only to the X and Y-axes of the camera, or its "right" and "up" vectors. The modifier keys for this constraint are 'Control' and 'X.'
  • XZ Axes Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement relative only to the X and Z-axes of the camera, or its "right" and "forward" vectors. The modifier keys for this constraint are 'Shift' and 'X.'
  • YZ Axes Constraint—Constrains the current camera interaction mode to movement relative only to the Y and Z-axes of the camera, or its "up" and "forward". The modifier keys for this constraint are 'Control' and 'X.'

Camera selection commands

Camera selection commands enable you to switch between cameras and change the properties of the current camera.

  • Previous Camera—Switches to the previous camera in the scene.
  • Next Camera—Switches to the next camera in the scene.
  • Zoom Extents—Positions the current camera so that the entire scene is visible.
  • Reset Camera—Resets the current camera to the position and rotation defined when the scene graph was loaded.

Rendering modes

The rendering modes shown here aid in identifying possible performance problems.

  • Wireframe mode—Toggles wireframe rendering ON/OFF.
  • Overdraw mode—Toggles overdraw rendering ON/OFF.

    The overdraw indicator uses the stencil buffer and screen-space elements to show the depth complexity of the scene. This can be used to determine how complicated the scene is from particular vantage points. There are four screen space elements corresponding to the colors blue, green, yellow, and red.

    • Blue—indicates a screen pixel that has had only one 3D polygon draw operation performed.
    • Green—indicates two polygon draws.
    • Yellow—indicates three polygon draws.
    • Red—indicates four or more polygon draws.
  • Toggle Wireframe ABVs—Toggles display of wireframe representations of alternate bounding volumes (ABVs) in the scene. Wireframe ABVs show up in blue.
  • Toggle node visualization—Toggles a graphical representation of the nodes in the scene which shows their position and orientation.
  • Toggle hierarchy visualization—Toggles a graphical representation of the hierarchical relationships between nodes. Parent nodes are connected to their children by a line, while nodes which affect skinned characters are shown as a sphere and a pyramid pointing in the direction of the bone.
  • Toggle bounds visualization—Toggles a graphical representation of the bounding spheres of mesh objects in the scene.
  • Reload Shaders—This button reloads all pixel and vertex shaders. This is useful for previewing changes made to NSF or FX files in the engine.

LOD modes

The LOD Modes shown here aid in previewing all possible LOD and Bone LOD settings.

  • LOD Preview Mode—Toggles LOD override on and off.
  • LOD Next Mode—Moves LOD override to the next level.
  • LOD Previous Mode—Moves LOD override to the previous level.
