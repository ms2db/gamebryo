Navigating Through the Scene in NifViewer

The NifViewer provides two navigation modes for changing your view of the scene, TURRET mode and TUMBLE mode. Toggle between the two modes by executing the Gamepad Tumble menu command.

Note

If "None" is listed in the Stick column, simply press the button listed in the Direction or Button column. Buttons are specified in the Gamebryo convention. To find the platform-specific equivalents, see one of the following pages: Windows, PS3, or XBox 360.

TURRET Navigation

The default navigation mode is TURRET. In this mode, you can translate and rotate the camera using the analog sticks according to the following table:

Stick Direction or Button Action
None L1 Move camera up
None L2 Move camera down
Left LEFT Move camera left
Left RIGHT Move camera right
Left UP Move camera forward
Left DOWN Move camera backward
Right UP Rotate camera up/Pitch Up
Right DOWN Rotate camera down/Pitch Down
Right LEFT Rotate camera left/Yaw Left
Right RIGHT Rotate camera right/Yaw Right

TUMBLE Navigation

The other navigation mode is TUMBLE mode. In this mode, the camera sits still and you can rotate the scene with the following actions:

Stick Direction Action
Left UP Rotate scene about camera UP vector
Left DOWN Rotate scene about camera UP vector
Left LEFT Rotate scene about camera RIGHT vector
Left RIGHT Rotate scene about camera RIGHT vector
Right UP Rotate scene about vector pointing in the same direction as the camera
Right DOWN Rotate scene about vector pointing in the same direction as the camera

Note that if the camera is attached as a child somewhere in the scene graph that is being rendered (as are the cameras in NIF files created by the Gamebryo 3ds max Plug-in), TUMBLE mode will not be useful. This is because any rotation that is applied to the scene will also be applied to the camera. The net effect of this is that the view of the scene remains unchanged. To use TUMBLE mode for such scenes, the you can select the NifViewer camera (View > Camera).
