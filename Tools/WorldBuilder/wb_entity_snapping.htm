Snapping Entities to the World

When working with entities in the world, sometimes it is helpful to perform transformations on them such that those transformations snap to discrete units. The snap toolbar contains the various settings and tools for setting up the snap coordinates and snap intervals to use when transforming entities.

Notes

When the various different transformation snap toggles are off, free placement mode is in effect. For example, if translation snap is turned off, entities may be translated without snapping to a designated interval.

Snap Points

Snap points are helpful tools to describe how entities should "snap" together. Users define snap points in their DCC tool of choice (Max, Maya, XSI) by creating a node named "SnapPoints" that is the child of the root node. Any children of this node will be recognized by the snapping sub-system. These snap points can be used when editing using the translation gizmo. When a single entity is selected, its snap points should become visible as green crosses. As the user drags the entity around the world, if any other snap points are in range, they too will become visible. When two snap points are within the snap tolerance of each other, the snap points will "snap" together. Note this will not affect the orientation of the object, just its position. Users can break the "snap" by either turning off snap points or by dragging the position outside of the snap radius.

When defining snap points in your DCC tool, make sure that they are tagged with NiOptimizeKeep. This will ensure the snap points are properly saved from optimization settings at export time.

Notes

Users may adjust the distance at which snap points "stick" by adjusting the document setttings WorldBuilder.SnapPoints.Snap Point Stickiness Radius.
