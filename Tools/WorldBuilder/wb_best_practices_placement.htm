Placing entities in the world

Entities can be placed in the world by simply dragging and dropping a placeable entity model from the Entity Palette. Once an entity has been instantiated in the world, it can be manipulated using the various transformation gizmos available in the tool.

World Builder uses the concept of "building block" meshes in order to construct a scene. Typically, artists will model pieces of a structure in such a way that each piece can be reused throughout the level. The structural pieces are also modeled in such a way that each piece is a certain size and is meant to fit together with other pieces. Below is a screen shot of two different "building block" pieces. The first is a wall segment and the second is a corner piece. These pieces can then be chained together to form a room. Using a consistent grid while designing a scene is helpful for fitting pieces together.

In some cases, one may need to add many entities in the scene with a random rotation and scale applied to them. For example, when placing a lot of tree meshes or rock meshes across a landscape, it becomes a huge workflow bottleneck to select each tree or rock mesh and manually adjust the scale and rotation. World Builder provides a tool called the Randomizer to assist with modifying the rotation and scale of multiple entities simultaneously.

Finally, when a scene consists of many thousands of entities, it becomes difficult to focus in on specific areas. Depending on the scene complexity, this many entities could also introduce tool time performance issues. Therefore, World Builder provides a means of hiding entities and freezing entities. Hiding entities allows the level designer to focus in on the area's he/she is interested in and freezing entities prevents accidental selection and manipulation of entities known to be in the final resting place in the scene.
